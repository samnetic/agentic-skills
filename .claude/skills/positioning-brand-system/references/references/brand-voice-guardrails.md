# Brand Voice Guardrails

## Voice Definition

Describe voice with behavior, not adjectives only.

| Dimension | Target | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Clarity | Short direct sentences | Overloaded jargon |
| Confidence | Specific claims with proof | Hype and superlatives |
| Tone | Practical and respectful | Snark, sarcasm, vagueness |
| Technical depth | Match audience level | Talking down or overexplaining |

## Do / Don't Examples

| Context | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | "Close your month-end in 2 days, not 7." | "Revolutionary AI-powered financial transformation platform." |
| CTA | "Start a 14-day pilot" | "Unlock the future now" |
| Feature copy | "Detect schema drift before ETL failures." | "Best-in-class robust architecture for all use cases." |

## Channel Calibration

| Channel | Voice Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Homepage | Outcome-first, concise |
| Pricing page | Transparent, concrete |
| Email | Conversational, direct CTA |
| Sales deck | Evidence-heavy and objection-aware |

## Quality Checklist

- Every value claim maps to proof.
- Every paragraph has one clear point.
- No empty adjectives without numbers or examples.
- CTA language indicates next concrete action.
