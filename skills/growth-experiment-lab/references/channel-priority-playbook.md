# Channel Priority Playbook

## Purpose

Choose experiment channels based on current bottleneck and signal quality.

## Channel Decision Table

| Bottleneck | Priority Channels | Typical Experiment Types |
|---|---|---|
| Low qualified traffic | SEO, partnerships, targeted outbound | New topic clusters, integration pages, partner offers |
| Low visit-to-signup | Landing page CRO, offer packaging | Headline/value proposition tests, CTA tests, social proof tests |
| Low activation | Onboarding, lifecycle messaging | Guided setup, checklist sequencing, activation nudges |
| Low paid conversion | Pricing/packaging, sales assist | Tier framing tests, trial constraints, pricing page clarity |
| Low retention | Product usage loops, success interventions | Habit loops, proactive support triggers, cancellation save tests |

## Selection Heuristics

- Prefer channels with shortest feedback loop when confidence is low.
- Prefer channels with strongest compounding effects for sustained growth.
- Avoid channel switching every cycle unless bottleneck changes.

## Monthly Cadence

1. Review funnel diagnostics.
2. Select top bottleneck.
3. Allocate 70% experiments to bottleneck, 20% adjacent, 10% frontier.
4. Review outcomes and rebalance.
